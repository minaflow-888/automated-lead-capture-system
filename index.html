Architecture

Three design decisions
that make it production-ready.

I didn't just connect tools — I designed logic that protects budget, keeps data clean, and surfaces problems before they reach the client.

Budget Protection Routing

Empty and spam submissions are caught at the first filter step — before consuming any automation operations. This alone saves up to 30% of monthly Make.com costs.

Data Standardization

All lead fields are normalized before storage — email formatting, whitespace, capitalization. Clean input from day one means no cleanup later and no duplicate records.

Self-Healing Error Visibility

Any failed step fires an instant Slack alert. The team sees the problem and can act before the lead ever notices something went wrong. No silent failures.

What I Learned

Building this taught me more
than any course could.

This wasn't just about connecting tools — it was about thinking through the full lifecycle of data, failure states, and user experience end-to-end.

01

Filter early, save money

I initially placed the spam filter after the data storage step. Moving it to step one reduced unnecessary Make.com operations significantly — a lesson in thinking about cost from the start, not after.

02

Error handling is not optional

A system without error alerts is a liability. I added the #tech-errors Slack route after realizing that a silent failure would be invisible — and a lost lead has a real business cost.

03

Data quality starts at input

Normalizing data at the parser stage — before it ever hits Google Sheets — made everything downstream cleaner. Garbage in, garbage out applies in no-code just as much as in software engineering.

04

Speed of response is a product decision

Under 10 seconds isn't a technical constraint — it's a business choice. I designed around the experience of the lead receiving the reply, not just the convenience of the team sending it.

What I'd build next: A two-way CRM integration (HubSpot or Notion), an auto-booking link in the reply email via Calendly, and a weekly digest report sent automatically to the business owner. The foundation is already in place to support all of this.
