THE PROBLEM

Manual lead handling creates friction fast

When lead capture depends on manual steps, the process slows down at the exact moment a business should be moving quickly. Data can be copied incorrectly, response times can drift, and the sales team loses visibility.

  • Leads arrive, but follow-up is not immediate
  • Important details can get lost between tools
  • Manual entry adds repetitive, low-value admin work
  • The team has no clean real-time overview

THE SOLUTION

A connected workflow built for speed and clarity

I designed a simple automation architecture that captures the lead through Webflow, routes the data through Make, stores it in a structured source of truth, and sends an internal team alert instantly.

  • One entry point for incoming leads
  • Automated routing instead of manual handling
  • Structured data storage for consistency
  • Immediate internal notification for visibility

VISUAL WORKFLOW

How the system works from submission to team alert

Each part of the workflow has a clear job. Together, they create a process that feels simple on the front end and dependable behind the scenes.

01

Lead capture starts on the website

The process begins with a Webflow form designed to collect lead details clearly and consistently. This keeps the entry point simple for the user and reliable for the business.

02

Automation logic handles the routing

Inside Make, the workflow processes incoming data, applies the correct logic, and pushes it through the system without manual intervention.

03

Data is stored in a clean central record

Submissions are logged in Google Sheets to create one structured view of incoming leads. This makes tracking, reviewing, and validating data much easier.

04

The team is notified instantly

A Slack notification gives the team immediate visibility, so no one has to wait for someone to manually forward important lead information.
